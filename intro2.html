I don't know if it's a memory or a dream, but there is this image in my mind—
faintly sitting on Grandpa's lap, holding a milk bottle, on a bus.
Bumping, swaying, moving on and on into eternity.


Grandpa told me:
when I was little, he held me on the bus,
and by the time it circled back home,
I still hadn't finished a bottle of milk.
"You never liked eating much, ever since you were small."

They told me it was Public Transport Line 198.


And there were also Line 70, Line 41, Line XX, Line XX…
From the vehicles Grandpa took when he left home,
to the ones we rode together,
to the ones that carried me back home—
twelve years of living together,
condensed into bus stops and bus routes.
Daily life turned into lines traced on a map.

In my mind, Line 198 seemed like it would never end.
But when I went looking for it again, grown up,
I found
that the route wasn't a loop after all.
It was a bus line with a final stop.
